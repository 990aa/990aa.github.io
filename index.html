Research

Fair Spectral Topology

Bridging Graph Theory, Optimization, and Algorithmic Fairness

Abstract

This research addresses the tension between geometric accuracy and demographic parity in unsupervised learning. By incorporating fairness constraints directly into the graph topology construction phase, this approach forces Laplacian eigenvectors to encode cross-group structures. This method eliminates the need for post-hoc corrections by ensuring clusters are balanced during the initial graph construction.

Key Contributions
  • Topological Intervention: Implements a fairness-aware k-nearest neighbor (k-NN) algorithm that balances node connections between same-group and cross-group neighbors, controlled by a fairness strength parameter $\alpha$.
  • Mathematical Framework: Connects graph theory (similarity graphs), optimization (spectral relaxation), and algorithmic fairness (disparate impact).
  • Computational Efficiency: Utilizes a sparse formulation and the Lanczos algorithm to achieve near-linear complexity, making it suitable for large-scale datasets.
  • Empirical Validation: Demonstrated on synthetic benchmarks to successfully achieve disparate impact ratios near 1.0 while maintaining high clustering quality.

Zero-Cost Decision Systems

Dynamic Programming vs Reinforcement Learning for Sequential Decision Making

Abstract

This project explores the "From Omniscience to Experience" narrative, positioning heuristic search, dynamic programming (DP), and reinforcement learning (RL) on a spectrum of required environmental knowledge. The research establishes a formal theoretical relationship between Bellman optimality equations and temporal difference (TD) updates, demonstrating how model-free learning approximates model-based computation.

Key Contributions
  • High-Performance Systems Architecture: Implemented in Rust using trait-based polymorphism to provide a unified benchmarking suite for $A^*$, Value Iteration, and Q-Learning.
  • Memory Optimizations: Developed a double-buffered value function for zero-allocation DP iterations and cache-friendly Q-table representations that achieve a 5x memory reduction compared to standard hash-based implementations.
  • Stochastic Analysis: Benchmarked algorithm performance in both deterministic and stochastic grid-world environments, quantifying the trade-offs between sample complexity and optimality guarantees.

Riemannian Spectral Dynamics

A Unified Framework for Understanding Neural Network Optimization

Abstract

This work develops a coherent theoretical framework built on differential geometry, spectral theory, and matrix decompositions to understand neural network training dynamics. It reframes the parameter space as a Riemannian manifold, providing insights into why specific optimization methods, like Natural Gradient Descent, are invariant to reparameterization.

Key Contributions
  • Geometric Perspective: Formulates the Fisher Information Matrix (FIM) as the natural metric tensor of the statistical manifold, establishing its fundamental connection to the expected Hessian.
  • Spectral Analysis: Analyzes local geometry near critical points through eigenvalue decomposition of the Hessian, deriving precise stability conditions for gradient descent based on the spectral radius.
  • Matrix Structural Dynamics: Employs Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) to track the "low-rank bias" phenomenon, where weight matrices concentrate energy into top singular modes during training to act as an implicit regularizer.
  • Loss Landscape Visualization: Provides experimental validation by computing and visualizing the spectral density of the Hessian at local minima to distinguish between flat and sharp minima.
